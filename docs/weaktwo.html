Weak Two-Bids

All suit openings on the two level, except 2♣ 1, are weak and natural. They show a preemptive holding, with a decent six-card suit, and insufficient points to open with a constructive (one-level) bid.

The quality of the opened suit varies with position and vulnerability — third-seat openings are the lightest, and second- and (especially) fourth-seat bids are more sound. Playing green against red, a third-seat 2♠ opening with only a jack 2 is possible.

Raises are only non-forcing bids. All other suit bids are natural and forcing for one round, and 2N is an artificial asking bid. A raise to game is two-way, made either with a strong hand that expects to make or furthering the preempt.

  • 2♥ — weak-two (6+♥, 11- HCP)
    • 2♠ — 5+♠, forcing
      • 3♥ — no ♠ support
    • 3♠ — 3+♠
    • 2N — 14+ HCP (Ogust)
    • 3♣ — 5+♣, forcing
    • 3♦ — 5+♦, forcing
    • 3♥ — to play
    • 4♥ — to play (two-way 3)

Ogust

The 2N response to a weak-two bid asks opener to describe his strength and suit quality. After opener describes his hand, responder can make a decision to sign off in opener's suit or 3N, or alternatively bid a natural suit (showing six cards and a very strong hand 4.

  • 2♥ — 2N (Ogust, 16+ HCP)
    • 3♣ — one ♥ honour, no extra values 5
    • 3♦ — two ♥ honours, no extra values
    • 3♥ — one ♥ honour, extra values
    • 3♠ — two ♥ honours, extra values
    • 3N — AKQ in ♥ 6

  1. The strong (20+ HCP) bid.↩

  2. Sometimes even a good five-card suit.↩

  3. Note that, unlike 4♥ and 4♠, 2♦ — 4♦ is always preemptive.↩

  4. This is not very useful with the structure described above. It would be much better to have new suits after a weak-two as moderately strong (14-16 HCP) but non-forcing, but the system currently says that they are forcing.↩

  5. "Extra values" meaning a side-suit honour.↩

  6. Responder should not expect any extra values, since opener would have bid 1♥ otherwise.↩
